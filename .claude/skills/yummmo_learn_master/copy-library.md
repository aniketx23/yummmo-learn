## 1. Brand Promise & Positioning

**One-line promise:** "Healthy baking jo ghar ki hai, ghar ke liye hai."
**Tagline variants:**
- "Swaad wahi. Guilt zero." ← punchiest
- "Jaggery pe banayi, pyar se sikhayi." ← warmest
- "25 saal ki maa ki kitchen wisdom, ab aapke phone par." ← transformation
- "Chini chhodni nahi, badalni hai." ← swap-focused
- "Atta, ragi, oats, gud — Yummmo Learn mein sab kuch ghar jaisa."

**Positioning vs alternatives:**
- vs YouTube free cooking channels: "Free toh hai, lekin step-by-step bina rukawat ke seekhna ho, aur seekhne ke baad support chahiye ho — yeh us liye hai."
- vs Udemy/general online courses: "Desi kitchen ke liye, desi ingredients ke saath, Hindi/Hinglish mein."
- vs celebrity chef courses: "Star chef nahi — aapki jaisi maa, jisne apne ghar mein yeh sab sach mein kiya."

**Core differentiator line (use this as the fallback hook):**
*"Har recipe mein ek healthy swap — maida ki jagah atta, chini ki jagah gud. Swaad wahi. Guilt zero."*

## 2. The Akta Story — Approved Framings

This is the brand's most authentic asset. Use it. Handle with care — do not embellish, invent credentials, or turn it into a pity story.

**The facts (do not deviate):**
- Akta Mahajan, 42
- 25+ years home cooking and baking experience
- Completely self-taught — no culinary school, no formal course
- Elder child weighed 120 kg in 2017
- Through Akta's food and lifestyle changes at home, child lost more than half body weight across 3 years
- Child now weighs 54 kg

**Approved framings (use any of these, don't invent new ones):**

*Short (for hero or card):*
> "Akta Mahajan ne apne bete ko 120 kg se 54 kg tak pahunchaya — tasty khaana khilate hue. Wahi recipes ab aapke liye."

*Medium (for about section on homepage):*
> "2017 mein Akta ke bade bete ka weight 120 kg tha. Unhone dieting ka rasta nahi chuna — ghar ki cooking badli. Maida ki jagah atta. Chini ki jagah gud. Ghee measured. Sab kuch homemade, sab kuch tasty. Teen saal mein bete ka weight 54 kg ho gaya. Ab wahi 25+ saal ki kitchen wisdom Yummmo Learn par."

*Long (for About page):*
> "Akta Mahajan 42 saal ki hain. Koi chef school nahi, koi culinary degree nahi — sirf 25+ saal ki home kitchen mein seekhi hui baat.
>
> 2017 mein unke bade bete ka weight 120 kg tha. Doctor, dietician, gym — sab try kiya. Lekin asli farak tab pada jab Akta ne ghar ki cooking badalni shuru ki. Cake atta ka banne laga. Mithai gud se. Snacks oats-ragi-multigrain. Sab kuch homemade, sab kuch eggless, sab kuch bina preservative.
>
> Teen saal baad bete ka weight 54 kg tha. Swaad ke saath.
>
> Yummmo Learn in hi recipes ka ghar hai — unhi swaps ka, jo ek ghar ne sach mein use kiye aur asar dekha."

**Never add:** fake awards, fake media features, fake celebrity clients, "featured on MasterChef", dietician/nutritionist credentials (she's not one), numeric weight-loss guarantees for students.

## 4. Course Detail Page — Copy Patterns

### Structure of a good course description

1. **One-line promise** — what student walks away with.
   *e.g. "Atta se banaya hua spongy dry cake — perfect chai ke saath."*
2. **Aapko kya seekhne ko milega** (3–5 bullets)
   - Sahi atta choose karna
   - Gud ko cake mein kaise use karein (lumps na ban ne dein)
   - Oven temperature + timing exact
   - Cake na bethe — is ka raaz
   - Storage: 4 din tak fresh
3. **Recipe ka healthy angle** — 2 lines on the swap story.
4. **Kiske liye hai** — 1 line on audience (new bakers / diabetic-friendly families / sweet-lovers cutting sugar).
5. **Duration & format** — "X videos • Y minutes • Hindi/Hinglish • Lifetime access."

### Short description (for cards, max 120 chars)

Formula: `[What] + [healthy angle] + [who]`
- "Atta dry cake — bina maida, bina chini. Chai ke saath perfect."
- "Ragi cookies jo bachhe bhi khushi se khayein — bina guilt."

### Level badges — when to use

- **Beginner** — Never baked before / first cake. Emphasize "ghabrayein nahi, step-by-step".
- **Intermediate** — Done basics. Ready for flavour variations, presentation, multiple swaps at once.
- **Advanced** — Fondant, multi-layer, event cakes, complex swaps (sugar-free + eggless + gluten-free at once).

## 7. CTAs — Full Library

**Primary (enroll/buy):**
- "Seekhna shuru karein" ← warmest
- "Enroll karein"
- "Abhi join karein"
- "Free mein try karein" (for free courses)
- "Buy ₹X" (direct)

**Secondary (explore):**
- "Courses dekhein"
- "Curriculum dekhein"
- "Free preview dekhein"

**Live class specific:**
- "Batch book karein"
- "Apna slot chunein"
- "Waitlist mein naam likhwayein"

**Newsletter / soft:**
- "Weekly recipes paayein"
- "Subscribe karein — har hafte ek healthy swap"

**Never use as CTA:**
- "Click here" ← dead phrase
- "Submit" ← cold
- "Unlock your potential" ← fake urgency

## 9. Forbidden Phrases (Hard No)

Never use these — they break the brand voice:

**Sterile corporate:**
- "Our comprehensive curriculum"
- "Master the art of..."
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Transform your life" ← yes, transformation IS the theme, but this phrasing is tired
- "Empowering learners"
- "World-class instruction"
- "Premium learning experience"

**Fake / over-claimed:**
- Any numeric weight-loss guarantee ("Lose 10 kg in 30 days")
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Doctor recommended" (she's not medical)
- "Nutritionist-approved" (she's not a nutritionist)
- "Diabetes cure" or "medical" claims
- Any language that suggests Akta has formal credentials she doesn't

**Cringe/tryhard:**
- "Level up your baking game"
- "Bake it till you make it"
- "Cooking goals"
- "Foodie life"

**Wrong register:**
- Pure formal Hindi ("Swasth pakvaan kala") — sounds like a textbook
- Over-translated English ("Let us embark on this delicious journey") ← translated from English brain
- Corporate English for surfaces that should be Hinglish

**Brand confusion:**
- Anything implying Yummmo Learn sells cakes/hampers (that's the parent Yummmo bakery)
- Anything implying parent Yummmo teaches courses (it doesn't — Learn is the education arm)

## 10. Variant Ladder Method

When generating copy, default to **3 variants** labeled:

- **Safe** — brand-voice compliant, minimal risk, good default
- **Warm** — leans harder into Akta's story and emotion; best for audiences new to the brand
- **Bold** — punchier hook, more Hinglish slang, riskier — use when audience is already warm

Example for a hero headline:

- **Safe:** "Healthy baking, ghar ki tarah — Hindi/Hinglish mein seekhein."
- **Warm:** "Akta ki 25 saal ki kitchen wisdom, aapke phone par."
- **Bold:** "Maida chhodo. Atta pakdo. Swaad wahi rahega — promise."

Always let the user pick. Don't pick for them unless they ask.
