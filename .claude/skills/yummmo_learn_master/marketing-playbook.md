## 1. Channel Strategy Overview

Priority order for Yummmo Learn (based on audience being Indian home cooks, 25–55, Hinglish):

**Tier 1 — where the audience already is**
- **WhatsApp** — Primary channel. Indians check WhatsApp 10x more than Instagram. Broadcasts, status, direct convos. Highest conversion to enrollment.
- **Instagram** — Discovery engine. Reels for reach, Stories for nurture, Posts for depth.

**Tier 2 — important supporting role**
- **YouTube** — SEO + long-form credibility. Yummmo Learn's "about" becomes more trustworthy when you can show 30+ cooking videos.
- **Blog (on the site)** — Already covered in SEO playbook. Organic discovery.

**Tier 3 — experimental / later**
- **Facebook** — Older audience, decent for ads targeting 40+ mothers. Not for organic.
- **LinkedIn** — Irrelevant for this audience.
- **Pinterest** — Sleeper potential for recipe pins driving India traffic. Low priority.

## 2. Content Pillars

Every piece of content maps to one of these four pillars. If it doesn't, it probably shouldn't be published.

### Pillar 1: The Swap (40% of content)
Concrete demonstrations of healthy substitutions. "Swap maida with atta in your next cake — here's how."
- Format examples: before/after reel, "don't do this, do this" carousel, quick swap video
- CTA: "Full recipe Yummmo Learn pe" or "Free preview dekhein"

### Pillar 2: The Recipe (30% of content)
A complete recipe — step-by-step, visual, usable. Free value. This is how trust is built.
- Format examples: recipe reel, carousel with ingredients + method, YouTube video
- CTA: soft — "Aur recipes chahiye? [link in bio]"

### Pillar 3: The Story (20% of content)
Akta's journey, behind-the-scenes, transformation posts, student wins.
- Format examples: Akta talking to camera, before/after photos of the weight-loss story (only with family consent), student testimonials
- CTA: "Akta ki full kahani padhein" → About page

### Pillar 4: The Teach (10% of content)
Tips, tricks, myths busted. Positions Yummmo Learn as the expert.
- Format examples: "3 mistakes in atta cake baking", "Why your cake bethta hai", myth-busting reels
- CTA: "Full course mein aur seekhein"

**Ratio matters.** If all content is Pillar 1 (Swap) and Pillar 4 (Teach), the feed feels salesy. If all Pillar 2 (Recipes), you're giving away everything for free and students don't enroll. 40/30/20/10 is the balance.

## 3. Instagram

### 3.1 Formats & their jobs

| Format | Job | Frequency target |
|---|---|---|
| **Reels** | Reach + discovery | 3–4/week |
| **Carousel posts** | Depth + saves | 2–3/week |
| **Stories** | Nurture existing followers | Daily (3–5 stories) |
| **Live** | High-trust conversion event | 1–2/month |
| **Static posts** | Rarely — only for announcements | As needed |

### 3.2 Reel hook library (first 3 seconds matter most)

**Pattern interrupts that work for this audience:**
- "Agar aap bhi apne bachhe ko healthy cake khilana chahti hain..."
- "Ye ek galti sab karte hain atta cake mein — *[show the mistake]*"
- "Maida chhodne ke baad pehla cake sab fail karte hain. Kyunki..."
- "Bete ka weight 120 kg se 54 kg — sirf ghar ka khaana badla."
- "Chini-free mithai — bachhe bhi khush, dada-dadi bhi khush."
- "Is cake mein chini nahi hai. Guess karo kya daala?"

**Reel formulas (proven structures):**

*Formula A — "Don't do this, do this":*
0–3s: Show the common mistake ("Maida + chini — skip it")
3–8s: Show the swap ("Atta + gud — try karein")
8–25s: Quick recipe
25–30s: CTA ("Full recipe Yummmo Learn pe")

*Formula B — Transformation reveal:*
0–3s: Cake looks like regular bakery cake ("Ye dekho")
3–8s: Reveal ingredients ("Lekin ye maida nahi, atta hai")
8–20s: Quick method
20–30s: Taste test / emotional hook

*Formula C — Akta talking head:*
0–3s: Direct to camera, personal line
3–25s: One specific tip or myth
25–30s: CTA

*Formula D — Student win:*
0–30s: Before/after of a student or family member + quote ("Pehle rozana cake nahi de sakti thi, ab de sakti hoon — kyunki ye healthy hai")

### 3.3 Hashtag library

**Rule:** Mix 3 tiers of hashtags — big, medium, niche. 15–20 total.

**Big (1M+ posts) — reach:**
#cooking #recipe #baking #indiancooking #foodie #instafood

**Medium (100k–1M) — discovery:**
#healthybaking #eggless #attacakes #homemadecake #indianrecipes #hinglishrecipes #noidafoodie #healthysweets

**Niche (under 100k) — conversion:**
#yummmolearn #attabaking #jaggerycake #ragicookies #healthyswapindia #eggslessbaking #homebaker #noidabaker #indianmomskitchen #healthyindiankidsfood #sugarfreebaking

**Local (if live classes are the post):**
#noida #delhi #delhincr #noidaclasses #delhibaking #noidacookingclass

### 3.4 Bio formula
**Line 1:** What you do — "Healthy baking, ghar ki tarah 🍰"
**Line 2:** Who teaches — "Akta Mahajan · 25+ saal · Hindi/Hinglish"
**Line 3:** Offer — "Online courses + In-person classes"

### 3.5 Story highlights (cover the buyer journey)

## 4. WhatsApp

### 4.1 WhatsApp Status

Post 3–5 statuses per day. Mix of:
- Behind-the-scenes of recipe testing
- Finished dish photo with 1-line caption
- Quick poll ("Next week: Dry cake ya brownie?")
- Akta voice note (30 seconds max)
- Student photos (with permission)
- Live class reminder when a batch is open

### 4.2 Broadcast lists

Segment the broadcast list:
- **Leads** — People who asked about courses but didn't enroll
- **Students** — Enrolled, receive content + community messages
- **Live Class Grads** — Received a class, most likely to come back
- **Warm** — Met Akta at an event or pop-up

Broadcast frequency: 2–3x/week max. One of them should be non-sales (just a free recipe or a tip). Sell-content ratio: 1 sales message to 3 value messages.

## 5. YouTube

### 5.1 Role of YouTube
Long-form credibility + SEO. A 10-minute tutorial that ranks on YouTube for "atta cake recipe" drives enrollments for 2 years. High-effort, high-return.

### 5.2 Format priorities

**Priority 1 — Long-form tutorials (10–15 min)** — Once a month is plenty. Full recipe + Akta teaching in-frame + CTA to course at end.

**Priority 2 — Shorts (under 60s)** — Repurpose Instagram Reels. 3–5 per week, minimal effort.

**Priority 3 — Live streams** — Monthly Q&A or live baking demo. Drives connection.

### 5.3 YouTube title formula
`{Keyword} — {Differentiator} | {Sub-benefit}`

Examples:
- "Atta Cake Recipe Eggless — Bina Oven Ke Kaise Banayein | No Maida No Chini"
- "Ragi Cookies for Kids — Tiffin Ke Liye Perfect | Healthy Recipe in Hindi"
- "Sugar-Free Gulab Jamun Recipe | Jaggery Use Karke | Diabetes Friendly"

## 6. Email Drip Sequences

For templates of individual emails see `copy-library.md` Section 8. Here's the sequence logic.

### Sequence A: Welcome → First Course (7 days)

- **Day 0** — Welcome email (on signup). Subject: "Namaste! Yummmo Learn mein swagat hai"
- **Day 1** — Free recipe in the email (atta brownie). Subject: "Ek recipe — mera tohfa"
- **Day 3** — Akta's story + link to About page. Subject: "120 kg se 54 kg — ek maa ki kahani"
- **Day 5** — Product nudge: feature one course. Subject: "Ye course aapke liye perfect hai"
- **Day 7** — Social proof: 2 student testimonials + offer. Subject: "Inhone try kiya — ab aapki baari"

### Sequence B: Cart / Browse Abandonment (3 days)

User viewed a course but didn't enroll:
- **Hour 2** — Soft reminder: "{Course} aapke cart mein intezaar kar raha hai" + link
- **Day 1** — FAQ-style: "2 baatein jo aam log poochhte hain is course ke baare mein"
- **Day 3** — Final touch: "Ek free preview lesson — dekh lein pehle"

### Sequence C: Post-Purchase Nurture (14 days)

- **Day 0** — Purchase confirmation
- **Day 1** — "Aapka pehla lesson kab shuru karoge?" + direct link to first lesson
- **Day 7** — Progress check: "Abhi tak kitna seekha? Koi sawal hai?"
- **Day 14** — Upsell gently: "Ye course pura hone par next step — {related course}"

### Sequence D: Live Class Post-Batch (3 touches)

After attending a live class:
- **Day 0 (evening of class)** — "Aaj ka lesson photos + recipe card"
- **Day 3** — "Ghar pe try kiya? Photo bhejo, status pe daalenge"
- **Day 7** — "Next batch {date} — dobara aana chahenge?" + online course link

## 7. Testimonials — How to Collect and Use

### The collection system

After any course completion or live class, send a WhatsApp message:
> "{Name}, {course/class} complete karne ke liye badhai! 🎉
>
> Ek chota sa request — kya aap apna experience 2 lines mein likh sakte hain? Aur ek photo apne dish ki bhej sakte hain? Isse naye students ko bharosa milega.
>
> Dhanyawad 🙏"

### What makes a testimonial usable

- Specific ("Mere bete ne pehli baar atta cake khaaya aur pasand kiya" — usable)
- Not generic ("Great course!" — not usable)
- With a name + city + optional photo
- With permission to share (ask explicitly)

### Where to use them

- Homepage testimonials section (3–5 rotated)
- Course detail pages (2–3 per course, matched to the course)
- WhatsApp status (1–2 per week)
- Instagram carousel (monthly)
- YouTube video end-cards

**Legal + ethical:** Never fabricate testimonials. Never use someone's name without permission. Never misrepresent the outcome.

## 8. Akta's Story — When to Use It, How Often

The 120-kg-to-54-kg transformation is the brand's best asset but can be overused and feel exploitative. Rules:

- **Homepage:** Use the Medium framing (copy-library Section 2) — always present.
- **About page:** Use the Long framing — always present.
- **Instagram:** Use the story fully once a quarter, as a Reel or carousel. Snippets more often, but the full story should feel special.
- **YouTube:** Once, as a pinned intro video. Don't repeat in every video.
- **Email Day 3 of welcome drip:** One-time share.
- **Sales pages for new courses:** Mention briefly, don't re-tell in full.

**Don't:**
- Don't turn it into a sympathy pitch.
- Don't show before/after photos of the child without the child's consent (now an adult).
- Don't pair it with "lose weight" promises — Yummmo Learn teaches healthy cooking, not weight-loss programs.
- Don't add fake precision ("Within exactly 1,095 days..." — 3 years is enough).

## 9. Paid Ads Guidance

**If/when paid ads are run**, here's the framework:

### Best-performing ad formats for this audience
1. **Instagram Reel ad** (short recipe + CTA) — best for cold audience
2. **Facebook carousel ad** (multiple recipes) — best for 40+ women audience
3. **Meta retargeting** (website visitors who didn't enroll) — best ROAS
4. **WhatsApp click-to-chat ad** (Instagram ad → WhatsApp conversation) — best for live class sign-ups

### Audience targeting presets

**Audience 1 — Cold mothers:**
Women, 28–50, India (Tier 1 + 2 cities), interests: cooking, baking, child nutrition, healthy food. Exclude: existing followers.

**Audience 2 — Warm re-targeting:**
People who visited yummmo.com + IG followers + video 50%+ viewers.

**Audience 3 — Live class geo:**
Women + Men, 25–55, within 20 km of Noida venue, interest: cooking.

### Ad copy principles

- Hook in first 3 words (same as reels).
- One CTA per ad.
- Image/video = dish photo, not graphic design.
- Include price explicitly if the product is under ₹1,000 ("₹499 mein" works well). Hide price if above ₹2,000 (drive to landing page first).

### Budget rule of thumb (starting)

- Test: ₹500–1,000/day per audience for 3 days
- If CPA (cost per enrollment) is under course price: scale
- If CPA is above course price: kill, rework creative

## 10. Campaign Calendar Framework

Three rhythms happen in parallel:

### Weekly rhythm
- Monday — Free recipe (WhatsApp + IG + Email)
- Tuesday — Swap tip (IG Reel + Story)
- Wednesday — Course spotlight (IG carousel + Email to leads)
- Thursday — Akta story snippet / BTS (IG + WA Status)
- Friday — Student win / testimonial (IG + WA)
- Saturday — Live class promo if batch coming up (WA)
- Sunday — Rest / Q&A / Community

### Monthly rhythm
- 1st week — New course launch OR new blog post
- 2nd week — Live class batch
- 3rd week — Email drip refresh / collection of testimonials
- 4th week — Review analytics, kill underperforming content, plan next month

### Seasonal rhythm (India-specific moments)
- **Raksha Bandhan (Aug)** — "Healthy mithai for your sister"
- **Ganesh Chaturthi (Aug/Sep)** — "Modak with jaggery"
- **Navratri (Sep/Oct)** — "Fasting-friendly bakes"
- **Diwali (Oct/Nov)** — Peak season. Hamper + mithai courses push hard. This is the #1 opportunity of the year.
- **Christmas (Dec)** — "Healthy Christmas cake" — eggless, atta, jaggery
- **New Year (Jan)** — "Healthy resolution" angle
- **Valentine's (Feb)** — "Healthy chocolate desserts"
- **Holi (Mar)** — "Gujiya with gud"
- **Birthdays (year-round)** — "Kids' healthy birthday cakes" evergreen

Plan one campaign per major moment, 3 weeks in advance.
