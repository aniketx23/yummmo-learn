## 1. SEO Philosophy For This Brand

Three rules that override everything else:

1. **Hinglish beats pure Hindi AND pure English.** Indian home cooks Google in Hinglish: "atta cake recipe", "eggless chocolate cake kaise banaye", "sugar free gulab jamun". Do not force pure Devanagari titles — you lose the long tail.

2. **Long-tail wins.** "Cake recipe" has 1M+ searches but you'll never rank. "Atta cake recipe without oven eggless" has 500 searches and you CAN rank. Chase long-tail clusters, not head terms.

3. **Recipe intent ≠ course intent.** "Oats cookies recipe" → user wants free steps (blog). "Learn baking online" → user wants a course (landing page). Don't cross-wire them — separate landing strategies.

## 2. How to Find the Right Keywords (Methodology)

Since the user specifically asked for this, here's the repeatable workflow. None of this requires paid tools.

### Step 1: Start with seed terms
Write down every phrase a student would Google. Examples for Yummmo Learn:
- "atta cake recipe"
- "sugar free dessert recipe"
- "eggless baking course"
- "healthy bakery items at home"
- "ragi cookies recipe"

### Step 2: Google autocomplete scrape (free, most important step)
For each seed term, type it into Google India (use an incognito tab, set location = India) and note every autocomplete suggestion. Then:
- Type the seed + each letter a–z (`"atta cake a"`, `"atta cake b"`, etc.) — reveals hidden completions.
- Type the seed + "kaise", "recipe", "banaye", "eggless", "without oven", "hindi", "for diabetes".
- Check "People also ask" and "Related searches" at the bottom.

Every unique completion is a real user query. Save them.

### Step 3: Free keyword tools (triangulate)
Use these to get rough volume/difficulty estimates. None is perfectly accurate, but they converge:
- **Google Keyword Planner** (free with a Google Ads account) — India-specific monthly search volumes. Best for "is this term searched at all" questions.
- **Ubersuggest** (free tier, 3 searches/day) — Gives volume + difficulty + related terms.
- **AnswerThePublic** (free tier) — Shows question-format queries ("how to", "why", "what"). Great for blog topic generation.
- **Keyword Surfer** (free Chrome extension) — Shows volumes right in Google search results. Highly recommended.

### Step 4: Competitor keyword mining
Take the top-ranking competitors for your seed term (ideally 3–5) and reverse-engineer:
- Open their page → look at their `<title>`, `<h1>`, `<h2>`s, URL slug, image alt text. Those are their target keywords.
- Paste their URL into Ubersuggest or Ahrefs free preview → see what they rank for.
- Top competitors in India for cooking content: Hebbar's Kitchen, Nisha Madhulika, Shipra Khanna's blog, Cook With Manali, Spice Up The Curry, Yummy Indian Kitchen.

### Step 5: Intent classification
For every saved keyword, tag it:
- **I** = Informational (user wants to read/watch) → blog post
- **T** = Transactional (user wants to enroll/buy) → course landing page
- **N** = Navigational (user is searching for you) → homepage
- **C** = Commercial investigation (user is comparing) → comparison blog or category page

Only chase keywords where intent matches what you can offer.

### Step 6: Prioritize
Build a spreadsheet: `keyword | monthly volume | difficulty | intent | page to target | priority (1–3)`. Priority logic:
- **P1:** Volume 100–2,000, difficulty under 30, clear intent match
- **P2:** Volume 2,000–10,000, difficulty 30–50, intent match
- **P3:** Head terms (volume 10k+, high difficulty) — only chase after 20+ P1 posts are ranking

### Step 7: Publish, then measure
Use **Google Search Console** (free) to see what Yummmo Learn actually ranks for. After 30 days of a page being live, check impressions/clicks/position. Iterate — expand content on what's ranking, deprecate what isn't.

## 3. Keyword Clusters — Starter Map

Six clusters based on what Yummmo Learn actually teaches. Each cluster is a group of related long-tail terms that a single page (blog or course) can target together. Volumes below are **rough estimates** — verify in Keyword Planner before finalizing.

### Cluster A: Atta / Whole Wheat Baking
**Core intent:** "How do I bake without maida?"
- atta cake recipe eggless
- whole wheat cake recipe in hindi
- atta cake without oven
- atta cookies recipe
- atta brownie recipe eggless
- bina maida ka cake
- healthy atta cake for kids

**Best page target:** Blog posts (informational) + one "Atta Baking Starter Course" landing page (transactional).

### Cluster B: Sugar-Free / Jaggery / Dates
**Core intent:** "How do I bake without refined sugar?"
- sugar free cake recipe
- jaggery cake recipe
- gud ka cake recipe

- bina chini ki mithai

**Best page target:** High-intent — diabetic audience searches these heavily. Strong blog + course potential. Be careful: do NOT make medical claims. "For people cutting sugar" ≠ "Cures diabetes".

**Core intent:** "How do I bake without eggs?" — huge Indian vegetarian market.
- eggless cake recipe
- eggless chocolate cake

**Best page target:** Eggless is the BIGGEST cluster for Indian audiences. Every recipe page should have "eggless" in the title if it actually is. This overlaps with every other cluster.

## 5. Meta Title + Description Formulas

### Meta title (max 60 chars)
**Formula:** `{Primary keyword} | {Modifier} | {Brand}`

Examples:
- `Atta Cake Recipe Eggless, No Oven | Yummmo Learn` (53 chars)
- `Ragi Cookies Recipe for Kids | Healthy | Yummmo Learn` (53 chars)
- `Baking Classes in Noida — Small Batch | Yummmo Learn` (52 chars)

Rules:
- Primary keyword in first 3 words
- Include one differentiator (eggless, healthy, for kids, sugar-free, no oven)
- Brand at end
- Never stuff keywords — one primary, max one secondary

### Meta description (max 155 chars)
**Formula:** `{Problem/hook} {Solution} {CTA/differentiator}`

Examples:
- `Maida ka cake chhodna chahte hain? Atta cake banayein — eggless, bina oven, bachhon ke liye safe. Step-by-step Hinglish mein.` (125 chars)
- `Ragi cookies recipe — bachhe khushi se khayein, bina guilt. 20 min mein taiyar. Akta Mahajan ki ghar-testede recipe.` (115 chars)
- `Noida mein small-batch baking classes — atta cake, healthy swaps, hands-on. Weekend + weekday batches available.` (112 chars)

### Course page meta patterns

- Title: `{Course name} | Hindi/Hinglish Course | Yummmo Learn`
- Description: `{What they learn in 1 line}. {Duration + format}. Akta Mahajan ki signature healthy swaps.`

### Open Graph title + description
Same as meta but can be slightly warmer/longer (OG has more room on social share cards). Always include an image with a dish photo + the recipe name in big text.

### Blog post structure (optimised for ranking)

Every post follows this skeleton:

1. **H1** — exact target keyword (e.g. "Atta Cake Recipe — Eggless, Soft, Without Oven")
2. **Intro (2–3 lines)** — hook with the problem (maida vs atta, refined vs healthy) + what they'll get by the end
3. **Why this recipe** — 1 paragraph on the swap story / who it's for
4. **Ingredients** — with exact measurements; call out swaps explicitly ("Maida ki jagah atta — use karein kyunki...")
5. **Method** — numbered, short sentences, verb-first (Voice B)
6. **Chef tip section** — 2–3 insider tips (this is what makes it rank + share)
7. **FAQs** — 3–5 Q&As targeting "People Also Ask" queries
8. **Related / next step** — "Ye pasand aayi? Akta ka full Atta Baking course dekhein →" (links to course)
9. **Schema markup** — Recipe schema (JSON-LD) for recipe posts. Boosts SERP features.

### Post frequency target

Realistic: **1–2 posts per week** for first 3 months, then 1 per week. Each post should live forever — evergreen is the strategy.

## 7. India-Specific SEO Considerations

1. **Voice search in Hindi is rising.** Voice queries skew conversational and longer — "ghar mein bina oven ke cake kaise banaye" vs typed "cake without oven recipe". Optimize headings to answer natural questions.

2. **Mobile-first is non-negotiable.** Most Indian users are on Android mobile with patchy 4G. Page must load under 2.5s on 3G throttled test. Image optimization (WebP, lazy loading) matters more than it does in US-SEO.

3. **Local intent for live classes.** "Baking classes near me", "cooking classes in Noida", "cake baking class Delhi NCR" — these require a Google Business Profile (free). Set one up for the physical venue of live classes. Embed Google Map on `/live-classes`.

4. **Multilingual pages — pick one primary.** Don't make Hindi + Hinglish + English versions of the same content fighting each other for the same keyword. Pick ONE primary language per page. Yummmo Learn's default: Hinglish for recipes, English for course landing pages, Hindi-heavy only for specific audiences.

5. **YouTube + Blog crosslink.** If Yummmo Learn does YouTube Shorts/videos, embed them on matching blog posts. YouTube content ranks in Google separately — doubles shelf space.

6. **WhatsApp-share-ability.** Recipe posts shared on WhatsApp get no backlink SEO benefit BUT huge direct traffic. Make titles + OG images "share-worthy" — simple, clear recipe photo, readable on a mobile lockscreen.

7. **Regional long-tail.** "cake in hindi" vs "cake in marathi" vs "cake recipe in tamil" — if Yummmo Learn decides to target regional queries later, create dedicated language versions. For now stick to Hinglish.

8. **Avoid generic Indian SEO mistakes:**
   - Don't stuff "best", "easy", "quick" in every title — Google penalizes.
   - Don't duplicate content across slugs.
   - Don't buy backlinks — India's SEO spam market is toxic.

## 8. Technical SEO Checklist

Already in the tech stack (Next.js App Router + SSR), so most of this is default. Verify:

- [ ] **Dynamic meta tags per course + blog page** — Confirmed in PRD Phase 6.
- [ ] **Sitemap.xml** — Auto-generate via Next.js. Include all published courses, blog posts, category pages.
- [ ] **robots.txt** — Allow indexing of public routes, disallow `/admin`, `/api`, `/auth`.
- [ ] **Canonical URLs** — Especially important for course slugs and category pages.
- [ ] **Structured data (JSON-LD):**
  - Course schema on `/courses/[slug]` pages
  - Recipe schema on recipe blog posts
  - Organization schema on homepage
  - FAQ schema where FAQ sections exist
- [ ] **OG images** — Dynamic OG image per course (dish photo + course name) and per blog post.
- [ ] **Image alt text** — Every image gets a keyword-rich alt text.
- [ ] **Core Web Vitals** — LCP under 2.5s, CLS under 0.1, FID under 100ms.
- [ ] **Internal linking** — Blog posts link to courses; courses link back to related blog posts.
- [ ] **Google Search Console verified** — Submit sitemap, monitor coverage.
- [ ] **Google Analytics 4** or PostHog — track which posts drive course enrollments.

## 9. What NOT to Do

- **No keyword stuffing.** One primary keyword per page. Natural prose.
- **No fake reviews / rich snippets.** Rating schema only if ratings are real.
- **No medical claims.** "Helps with diabetes" / "cures obesity" — never. Google's YMYL (Your Money Your Life) policy will tank the site.
- **No cloaking / hidden Hindi text.** Some Indian SEO shops still do this — instant penalty.
- **No AI-generated filler posts.** Google's March 2024 and later updates heavily penalize low-quality AI content. Every blog post should have Akta's voice and real recipe testing behind it.
- **No duplicate content across "atta" and "whole wheat" versions of the same recipe.** Pick one slug, include both terms naturally in the post.
- **No "click here" anchor text.** Waste of link equity.
- **No orphan pages.** Every published page should be linked from somewhere else on the site.
